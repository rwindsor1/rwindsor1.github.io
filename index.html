A short bio

That's me on the right. I was born in 1996 in Atlanta in the USA but my parents moved back to the UK and so I grew up in the town of Shrewsbury. At secondary school I was given a scholarship to Wrekin College, where I developed my love of sport, in particular rugby and running. I was also elected Head Boy during my final year. Due to some fantastic teaching, I decided I wanted to study physics at university and was offered a scholarship at the University of Manchester for my A level results.

Over the course of my degree, I have developed an interest in machine learning, aritificial intelligence and statistical science, which was cemented by my summer internship (see selected projects). I am hoping to pursue further study applying machine learning in real-world systems to generate insights and develop intelligent software and machinery.

Selected Projects

Here's a few things I've been working on

meet-me-halfway.net

Made by myself and a few friends during the 2017 Great Uni Hack, this website calculates the cheapest place for you and a friend on the other side of the world to meet. This is still a work in progress (the website isn't even hosted yet!).

Learn More

Lung Cancer Shrinkage

My MPhys project performed in collaboration with George Needham , we are working at the Christie Cancer Research centre in Manchester, exploring the link between lung cancer tissue density during radiotherapy and the likelihood of relapse.

Learn More

SecCam footfall mapping

My summer research internship, performed for the Victoria and Albert Waterfront in Cape Town, South Africa. I used computer vision techniques on security camera footage from the Waterfront to create a system to count the number of pedestrians passing through the area. This project was my first real experience of applying machine learning to a real-world problem.
