city guesser

That's me there. I hail from a medium sized town in the West Midlands called Shrewsbury but nowadays I live in Oxford where I'm a postgraduate DPhil student researching the use of artifical intelligence in the treatment of cancer. I'm funded by Cancer Research UK.

Over the course of my degree, I have developed an interest in machine learning and artificial intelligence in particular as a means of making intelligent systems (robots, expert systems and all that other cool stuff). I set up this website as a place to put stuff I've been working on. When I've got a little more time I'm hoping to set up a blog too. If you want to contact me you can email me at rhydian@robots.ox.ac.uk, or twitter me. Or you could have a look at my CV if you're so inclined.

Automated Footfall Mapping

My summer research internship, performed for the Victoria and Albert Waterfront in Cape Town, South Africa. I used computer vision techniques on security camera footage from the Waterfront to create a system to count the number of pedestrians passing through the area. This project was my first real experience of applying machine learning to a real-world problem.
