A short bio

That's me in the middle. I hail from a medium sized town in the West Midlands called Shrewsbury but nowadays I live in Manchester, where I studied a Master's in Physics. Next year (graduation permitting!) I'll be starting as a postgraduate DPhil student at the University of Oxford researching the use of artifical intelligence in the treatment of cancer, funded by Cancer Research UK .

Over the course of my degree, I have developed an interest in machine learning and artificial intelligence in particular as a means of making intelligent systems (robots, expert systems and all that other cool stuff). I set up this website as a place to put stuff I've been working on. When I've got a little more time I'm hoping to set up a blog too. Predicting Hurricanes Using Deep Learning

A keras tutorial for complete beginners, written for a JBCA Hacknight. Also includes an introduction to the python library basemap and a pretty cool historical dataset of hurricanes and weather conditions around Florida.

Lung Cancer Shrinkage

My MPhys project performed in collaboration with George Needham , we are working at the Christie Cancer Research centre in Manchester, exploring the link between lung cancer tissue density during radiotherapy and the likelihood of relapse.

Automated Footfall Mapping

My summer research internship, performed for the Victoria and Albert Waterfront in Cape Town, South Africa. I used computer vision techniques on security camera footage from the Waterfront to create a system to count the number of pedestrians passing through the area. This project was my first real experience of applying machine learning to a real-world problem.
