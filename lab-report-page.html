SecCam footfall mapping

My summer research internship, performed for the Victoria and Albert Waterfront in Cape Town, South Africa. I used computer vision techniques on security camera footage from the Waterfront to create a system to count the number of pedestrians passing through the area. This project was my first real experience of applying machine learning to a real-world problem.

Electrical Properties of Graphene

Another third year laboratory project. Here we explore the electrical properties of graphene using the method Andre Geim and Konstantin Novoselov used in their Nobel Prize winning paper "Electric Field Effect in Atomically Thin Carbon Films"

Inflation and the Second Law of Thermodynamics

An essay I wrote for a final year physics and philosophy course concerning the apparent contradiction between inflation's 'generic' conditions and the second law of thermodynamics pointing to highly specialised initial conditions.
